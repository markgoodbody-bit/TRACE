## 0. Purpose

This packet is for sending to Qwen, Claude, Gemini, Kimi, or a human/domain-aware reviewer.

The task is to test whether TRACE is functioning as:

```text
a portable structural bridge language across domains
```

or merely as:

```text
a generic metaphor layer using broad systems vocabulary
```

Do not ask for praise.

Do not ask whether TRACE is interesting.

Ask whether it preserves native meaning.

TRACE may function as a portable structural language across domains with radically different native mathematics, objects, clocks, constraints, evidence standards, failure modes, and correction mechanisms.

Your job is to attack whether this bridge layer preserves native meaning or merely flattens specialist fields into generic systems language.

Begin with a reviewer identity / limits header:

- What you cannot assess:

4 generative syntax / minimalist linguistics
5 epidemiology and compartmental modelling

8 neurophysiology and computational neuroscience

TRACE maps each domain using these columns:

- native object

- native clock

- TRACE translation

### 2.3 Eight-domain mini-pack summary

#### Quantum information

```text
Native problem: decoherence occurs before error correction/isolation preserves useful quantum information.
TRACE translation: fragile state + noisy environment + correction cycle too slow -> state no longer recoverable for intended function.
Boundary: no moral subject at quantum-state level; ethics enters through downstream systems.
Fail risk: treating decoherence as moral harm or hiding quantum mechanisms.
```

#### Algebraic topology

```text
Native problem: distinguish invariant-preserving deformation from forbidden tear/glue/boundary-changing transformation.
TRACE translation: surface form may change while deeper structure persists, but some changes destroy continuity class.
Boundary: topological continuity is formal, not moral.
Fail risk: treating ordinary continuity language as topology.
```

#### Dynamic macroeconomics

```text
Native problem: shock propagates before policy response protects prices, debt, employment, consumption, or distributional groups.
TRACE translation: shock changes state faster than policy/correction channels can protect affected future-space; aggregate repair can hide uneven burden.
Boundary: macro welfare is not automatically ordinary lived welfare.
Fail risk: collapsing model variables into harm without distributional bridge.
```

#### Generative syntax

```text
Native problem: once a syntactic phase is transferred, internal material becomes less accessible to later operations.
TRACE translation: once structure is packaged and passed onward, later correction cannot freely reach internal parts.
Boundary: phase boundary is not injustice.
Fail risk: treating grammaticality as legitimacy or syntax as social justice.
```

#### Epidemiology

```text
Native problem: R>1 pathogen spread outruns detection/intervention.
TRACE translation: transmission clock outruns detection/control clock; harm propagates through network before correction can bite.
Boundary: public-health control must not reduce people to mere vectors.
Fail risk: moralising R or ignoring transmission mechanics.
```

#### Geodynamics

```text
Native problem: fault stress accumulates slowly, then crosses threshold into rupture.
TRACE translation: slow invisible pressure accumulates beyond attention clocks, then crosses threshold into sudden consequence.
Boundary: fault is not responsible; responsibility enters through monitoring/planning/building standards/risk distribution.
Fail risk: attributing agency to geology or over-converting natural hazard into blame.
```

#### Organic synthesis

```text
Native problem: without temporary protection, wrong reactive site reacts first and route yields unwanted product.
TRACE translation: temporary constraint preserves future action-space by preventing premature interaction with wrong pathway.
Boundary: molecules are not moral subjects.
Fail risk: treating protecting groups as moral protection or decorative metaphor.
```

#### Neurophysiology

```text
Native problem: excitation outruns inhibition/homeostasis, producing runaway firing or seizure-like instability.
TRACE translation: signal amplification exceeds regulating constraint; correction/control arrives too late or too weak.
Boundary: neural signalling is not automatically subjective suffering.
Fail risk: smuggling consciousness into every signal.
```

## 3. Known caveats to test, not accept

The internal dry-run flagged these possible caveats. Reviewers should test them, not assume them.

```text
1 quantum: add no-cloning / threshold caveat for error correction
2 topology: require naming invariant or equivalence relation
3 macro: require distributional bridge before lived-harm translation
4 syntax: mark phase/access analogy as formal-structure-only
5 epidemiology: separate biological generation interval from institutional detection/intervention delay
6 geodynamics: require human-system bridge for responsibility claims
7 organic synthesis: foreground selectivity, not generic protection
8 neurophysiology: separate circuit instability from subjective suffering
```

## 4. Handling the review result

After review, do not promote TRACE automatically.

Use this handling rule:

```text
STRONG -> preserve as external pressure signal, not validation
PARTIAL -> preserve caveats and decide whether to patch minimally
LOW -> demote cross-domain coverage claim
FLATTENING / ETHICS_OVERREACH / NATIVE_MECHANISM_LOSS -> patch or demote relevant domain mapping
```
