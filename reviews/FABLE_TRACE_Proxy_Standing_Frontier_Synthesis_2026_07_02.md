## 1. Strongest current claim

This asymmetry is the spine of the proxy-standing grammar.

Fable's compression:

```trace
proxy := brake + bell + record
```

A proxy may object, advocate on both loss and opportunity clocks, delay before irreversibility, raise D-omission, create a contestable record, and escalate. A proxy may never consent, clear, close D, or discharge residue.

## 2. Proxy-standing v0.2

```trace
proxy_standing_v0_2 :=
  provisional_channel_for(absent | voiceless | unknown | future | dead | disputed_scope)
  + duty_direction(toward_scope, not_toward_process_completion)
  + powers(object + advocate_on_both_clocks + delay_before_T_irr
           + raise_D_omission + create_contestable_record + escalate)
  - powers(consent | clearance | close_D | discharge_residue)
  + forced_routing_to(constraint_layer + designation_layer)
  + interpolation_gap_declared
  + proxy_contamination_record(selection + payment + scope_setting + incentives)
  + subordination(any_direct_voice_outranks_proxy_immediately_and_prospectively)
  + obsolescence_duty(build_toward_direct_standing_where_theoretically_possible)
  + expiry_and_recheck(clocked_at_creation)
  - care_certification
  - final_designation
  - substitution_for_the_party
```

Plain English:

A proxy is a brake, a bell, and a record; never a signature. It may object, urge, delay, and force the question open. It may never consent, clear, settle who counts, or close the books on loss. It must declare what it cannot know. It is outranked if the real party can speak. Where direct standing could ever be built, the proxy must work toward its own retirement.
