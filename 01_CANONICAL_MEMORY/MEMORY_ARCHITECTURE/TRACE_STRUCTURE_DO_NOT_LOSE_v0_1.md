2. Case legibility is not proof.
3. AI agreement is not validation.
4. Relay packs are compatibility artifacts, not source canon.
5. Bootstraps teach operators; they are not the operators.
6. Operators are the memory spine.
7. Claims must be demotable or removable.
8. Kernel branches must be earned by pressure, not added because a case is powerful.
9. K-gate must distinguish nominal presence from functional linkage or it becomes tautology.
10. Exit-route branch must not become generic anti-institutionalism.
11. Protective secrecy must not become opacity permission.
12. Groundhog Day does not prove free will.
13. Frankenstein does not prove AI personhood.
14. Creator responsibility does not mean total control forever.
15. Recursive agency does not guarantee moral progress.
16. Mandelbrot is a structural analogy only when rule/iteration/boundary/outcome are explicit.
17. Care with teeth must preserve agency; care must not become control.
18. Repair must restore real future-space, not just symbolic closure.
19. Pre-registered tests are needed to move beyond post-hoc interpretation.
20. If TRACE cannot lose claims, TRACE becomes a belief system.
